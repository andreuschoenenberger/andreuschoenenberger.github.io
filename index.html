Short bio

Statistician with solid background and more than 5 years of experience in Clinical Trials, Research, and Data Science. I am passionate about clinical data, biomarkers, statistical methods, and innovation to bring the best treatment outcomes to patients, always learning in the process and thinking out of the box.

  • Experienced in Phase I-IV, and RWE in several therapeutic areas.
  • Knowledge of CDISC and seasoned in CRO oversight.
  • Extensive know-how in project leadership with strong interpersonal skills.

My background

Born in Lleida, Spain closely after the 1992 Barcelona Olympics. I hold a Bachelor of Science (BSc) in Physics from Universitat de Barcelona (UB), where I specialized in Applied Physics, particularly involving statistical modeling of physical phenomena. I pursued a Master of Science (MSc) in Statistics and Operational Research in 2016 from Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC, BarcelonaTech) with an Erasmus+ stay in University of Copenhagen (UCPH) at the end of 2017 where I did my Master Thesis under mentorship of Prof. Per Kragh Andersen.

Summary of my experience

I began my statistical career in 2016 while enrolled in my Master's in Statistics, working for a Gastroenterology research group in Vall d'Hebron Institut de Recerca (VHIR). There, I supported statistical analyses of various types of biological data, with a focus on gut microbiome (bacterial RNA sequence data) and its correlation with clinical outcomes. We published several papers, and I also contributed to the proposal of biomarkers for Chron's Disease (DOI: 10.1136/gutjnl-2016-313235). Later, I moved to Copenhagen to conduct research in Survival Analysis and Modeling of pseudo-observations with covariate dependent censoring under the supervision of Per Kragh Andersen. This work involved R programming, scientific writting, and a strong emphasis on Monte Carlo Simulation to derive results for specified methods. You can read my Master Thesis here.

From 2018 to 2020, I worked for TFS HealthScience and was involved in several clinical trials in different phases of development, notably Dermatology and Neurology. I also supported Almirall in the statistical tasks related to their Medical Affairs, publications, and HTA and reimbursement submissions. Overall, I specialized in longitudinal data analysis (e.g., Mixed Effects model and repeated measures) and missing data imputation (e.g., Multiple Imputation).

In 2020 I relocated to Prague to work for Sotio Biotech, initially leading a Phase 1 and Phase 2 trial in Immuno-Oncology. Since the beginning of 2023 I have been leading statistics at a clinical program level, encompassing several trials in different phases of development, with a focus on data-driven decisions for the clinical development plan and its strategy while also reporting results to Senior Management and partnering with other pharma companies for a joint development. For this purpose, I have led the data integration from different domains, working cross-functionally, including Pharmacodynamic, Pharmacokinetic, and Clinical outcomes. I have also participated in innovation initiatives such as Bayesian sample size calculation, BOIN design for early-phase studies, calculated predictive probabilities of study success, and developed an RShiny application for internal company data read-outs and status. Additionally, I have contributed to standardizing statistical and programming methods (including CDISC) across the company for Oncology analyses and censoring rules.
